# Content Preservation Rules

## Important

The redesign is a VISUAL + STRUCTURAL modernization.

It is NOT a complete content rewrite.

Do NOT randomly remove destinations, packages, or major sections from the existing website.

## Rules

1. Preserve all important destinations from the old website.

2. Preserve all major travel categories:
- Visit Argentina
- Golf
- Caribbean
- Europe
- USA
- Rest of the World

3. Preserve important itineraries and golf destinations.

4. Do NOT delete content unless it is:
- duplicated
- broken
- placeholder text
- clearly low-quality spam/test pages

5. If simplifying content:
- summarize it
- reorganize it
- improve readability
- improve hierarchy
- improve layout

BUT:
- do not remove meaningful travel offerings.

6. Maintain logical destination order and grouping.

Example:
Argentina destinations should remain grouped together:
- Buenos Aires
- Iguazú
- Bariloche
- El Calafate
- Ushuaia
- Mendoza
- Puerto Madryn
- Salta

7. Preserve all golf regions and major international destinations.

8. Old content folders are REFERENCE ONLY:
- sitepro
- golf 2

Never modify or delete files inside those folders.

9. Only create/edit files inside:
redesign/

10. Always COPY assets into:
redesign/assets/

Never move or delete original assets.

11. Focus on:
- cleaner UX
- modern presentation
- luxury visual design
- reusable templates
- easier navigation

NOT reducing the business offerings.

12. Before removing any section, verify:
- it is duplicated
- outdated
- broken
- or intentionally excluded.

13. Prioritize content organization over content removal.

14. The redesign should feel:
- curated
- premium
- cinematic
- organized

while still representing the full business offering.

15. Keep the site easy to maintain:
- reusable layouts
- reusable cards
- reusable sections
- simple static HTML structure
